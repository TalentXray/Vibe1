Vibe Coding

The recruitment industry has been buzzing with talk of AI, automation, and the future of hiring. For months I've been watching the hype build — tools that write job specs, screen candidates, predict attrition, and apparently do half my job for me. So I figured: rather than just read about it, why not actually try it? This page is the result of me sitting down with an AI, describing what I wanted, and letting it build. No code editor. No developer. Just conversation and curiosity. Welcome to my first experiment in vibe coding.

What is this?

The AI hype is loud.
I decided to test it myself.

Every conference, every LinkedIn post, every industry newsletter — it's all AI, all the time. The recruitment sector is no exception. Platforms are racing to embed machine learning into ATS systems, sourcing tools, and candidate matching engines. The promise is compelling: faster hiring, reduced bias, better outcomes. But how much of it actually works? And more interestingly — could someone with zero technical background actually build something with these tools?


Vibe coding is the idea that you describe what you want in plain language, and an AI writes the code for you. No syntax. No Stack Overflow. No developer needed. I thought it sounded too good to be true. So I tried it. This page — the design, the layout, every line of code — was generated through conversation with Claude. I'm a recruiter, not an engineer. And here we are.

The real opportunity

Use cases for vibe coding
in modern recruiting

Beyond building websites, the same principle — describe what you need, let AI build it — opens up a surprising range of possibilities for recruitment teams who've never written a line of code.

🎯

Custom Candidate Scorecards

Build bespoke scoring tools for specific roles — no spreadsheet expertise needed. Describe the competencies, weights, and red flags, and get a working interactive scorecard in minutes.

📋

Job Description Generators

Move beyond generic templates. Vibe code a tool that asks smart questions about the role and outputs a compelling, inclusive, on-brand JD tailored to biotech and life sciences.

📊

Hiring Pipeline Dashboards

Build a real-time visual dashboard for your hiring funnel without touching your ATS. Connect it to a spreadsheet and track stage conversion, time-to-offer, and drop-off rates.

💬

Interview Question Banks

Create a searchable, filterable library of competency-based interview questions organised by seniority, function, and therapeutic area — built entirely through conversation with AI.

🤝

Candidate-Facing Microsites

Build bespoke landing pages for senior or confidential searches — giving candidates a curated, branded experience without the overhead of a full design project.

⚗️

Offer Comparison Calculators

Help candidates weigh up competing offers with a custom tool that factors in salary, equity, benefits, location weighting, and career trajectory — built to your spec.

📧

Outreach Sequence Builder

Design and test multi-touch outreach campaigns with AI-assisted copy, personalisation logic, and timing recommendations — without needing a CRM developer.

🔬

Market Mapping Tools

Build lightweight competitor talent maps for specific functions or geographies. Describe the parameters, and get a structured, visual output you can share with clients.

📁

Onboarding Resource Hubs

Create beautiful, organised microsites for new joiners — consolidating culture docs, team intros, 30-60-90 day plans, and FAQs into one polished experience.

The honest bit

This is my first attempt.
It won't be my last.

Building this page took an afternoon. Not because it was hard — because I kept tweaking things just because I could. The barrier between "I have an idea" and "that idea exists on the internet" has never been lower. For recruiters who want to build tools for clients, create better candidate experiences, or just understand what's actually possible with AI — vibe coding is worth exploring. I'm sharing this experiment publicly because the best way to learn is to do it in the open. Follow along as I keep building.
